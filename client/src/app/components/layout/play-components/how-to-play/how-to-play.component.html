How to use Live Capture

During live capture, tetrisfish processes NES Tetris gameplay fed by an external source in real time. It communicates with the engine StackRabbit to give you near-instant feedback as you play. We store all your games, so you can examine any of your past games with our full suite of analysis tools!

1. Set up your source

You can screen capture directly from a NES Tetris emulator on your computer, or connect from your NES console through a capture card to your computer as a video source. You should be able to see your selected video output on the screen.

2. Calibrate your game

Click on an empty part of the board, and tetrisfish will intelligently calibrate itself and extract the Tetris board directly from your video source! Verify everything looks correct on the right. In rare cases, you might have to fiddle with the settings on the left panel to ensure a good read.

3. Start playing!

Once you are happy with calibration, click “Back to recording” on the top left. You can immediately start playing games, and tetrisfish will automatically detect when you start. Try to aim for a high accuracy when you play! After each game, you can click “Analyze” to review how you did!
